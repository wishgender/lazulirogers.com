## The Backstory

For their Thursday, September 25th episode, ["CATS OUT OF THE BAG"]("https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=QNX3-g62QIA"), BSE's *Grab Bag* needed someone to fill in for one of their floor crew. I had never done floor crew for an entertainment show (only ever on newscasts), much less a scripted sketch comedy show like *Grab Bag*, so I wasn't sure what to expect. On top of that, I'd never actually seen an episode of *Grab Bag* produced, only clips from previous episodes.

## Setting Up

When I arrived, the cast was already in the studio, setting up the set and props. That was a very different experience than what I'm used to, as on newscasts, talent rarely arrives in the studio before half an hour to showtime, and on the other entertainment shows, the cast comes downstairs from the entertainment office at around the same time as the crew's arrival, if not a little later.

It definitely makes sense for *Grab Bag*, though, as the show's cast members are the ones writing the scripts, making the props, and acting the scenes. The biggest responsibility for floor crew really seemed to be ensuring the cameras were exactly as the showrunners wanted. The camera placement was essentially done before I entered the studio, since the other floor crew, Noah Beitzel, had handled it himself. At one point, one of the showrunners, Wyatt Simon, had me help him with the lighting, since the shadows were too stark against the whiteboard in the background.

## Comparison with Previous Experiences

In my experience as floor crew for the Friday newscast last spring, manning the cameras was definitely important, but what I was most often doing was making sure all the anchors were in the right place at the right time. With *Grab Bag*, the cast who would be appearing in the live sketch already knew where they needed to be and when. The only cues I needed to give them was when to start the scene.

This episode only had one live sketch and two pieces of digital content, but other episodes have two live sketches and one piece of digital content. That certainly contributed to the experience being less stressful, given that we would have had to move the cameras around a lot more in order to prep for the second sketch.

## Final Thoughts

Overall, this experience has me considering joining the cast or crew of *Grab Bag* when the spring semester rolls around. It was a lot of fun to finally see this show in action, and I'm looking forward to future episodes.
